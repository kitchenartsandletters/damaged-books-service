Damaged Books Service

Staff How-To & Rules Guide

⸻

What This Is

The Damaged Books Service is the system we use to safely sell books that arrived with cosmetic damage (light, moderate, or heavy) without breaking our main catalog.

Instead of editing the original product or creating ad-hoc listings, this service:
	•	Creates separate damaged product listings that point back to the original book
	•	Tracks inventory by damage level
	•	Applies standardized discounts
	•	Ensures damaged sales do not contaminate normal inventory, pricing, or reporting

This system exists to protect:
	•	Catalog integrity
	•	Inventory accuracy
	•	Customer experience
	•	Future reporting and automation

Shopify does not provide a built-in safeguard to prevent duplicate or conflicting product listings. This means Shopify will happily allow multiple products to exist that represent the same book—even if doing so creates pricing conflicts, inventory mismatches, or customer confusion.

How Damaged Listings Are Created (Important)

How It Actually Works

Damaged book listings are created by the system, not by hand.

When damaged stock is identified:
	1.	A damaged product is generated that:
	•	References the original (canonical) product
	•	Has variants like:
        •	Light Damage
        •	Moderate Damage
        •	Heavy Damage
	2.	Each variant has:
	•	Its own inventory count
	•	Its own discount level
	3.	The damaged product automatically:
	•	Links back to the original book
	•	Displays standardized damage explanations
	•	Shows standardized discount messaging

What You Must NOT Do

Do not:
	•	Create a damaged product manually in Shopify
	•	Duplicate an existing product and “edit it into” a damaged version
	•	Create a damaged listing by copying another damaged product
	•	Change a normal product into a damaged one

These actions will break:
	•	Inventory tracking
	•	Pricing logic
	•	Reporting
	•	Navigation logic

If a damaged product does not exist yet, do not attempt to create it yourself.

⸻

How Inventory Is Allowed to Be Updated

What You ARE Allowed to Do

You may adjust inventory only for existing damaged variants:
	•	Increase quantity if more damaged copies are found
	•	Decrease quantity if copies are removed or discarded
	•	Set quantity to zero when sold out

This is done per damage level (variant), not on the product as a whole.

Examples:
	•	Light Damage → 12 copies
	•	Moderate Damage → 0 copies
	•	Heavy Damage → 0 copies

This is expected and safe.

⸻

What You Must NOT Do

Do not:
	•	Edit the price of a damaged variant manually
	•	Change discount percentages
	•	Rename damage levels
	•	Add new variant types
	•	Change inventory on the original (non-damaged) product to reflect damage

Discounts and messaging are controlled centrally and must stay consistent.

Product Descriptions & Copy (Read Carefully)

What Is Automatic

For damaged books, the system automatically handles:
	•	Damage explanation text (based on variant selected)
	•	Discount messaging
	•	A link back to the original book
	•	Return and final-sale messaging

This ensures:
	•	Customers are properly informed
	•	Language stays consistent
	•	Legal and policy language is respected

⸻

What You Must NOT Edit Manually

Do not:
	•	Edit the damaged product description
	•	Add custom damage notes
	•	Add staff commentary (“this one is slightly worse”)
	•	Remove or rewrite the damage explanation
	•	Add extra disclaimers

If something seems unclear or incorrect, flag it — don't fix it in Shopify.

⸻

Pricing & Discounts (Why You Shouldn’t Touch Them)

Each damage level has a standard discount:

Damage Level	Discount
Light	15% off
Moderate	30% off
Heavy	60% off

These are enforced by the system and reflected dynamically on the product page.

Do not:
	•	Manually set prices
	•	Add compare-at prices
	•	Apply additional discounts
	•	Run promotions on damaged products

Doing so causes mismatches between:
	•	Displayed discount language
	•	Actual prices
	•	Reporting and reconciliation

⸻

Navigation & Visibility (Why Some Links Appear or Disappear)

Damaged books may appear in a Damaged Books collection.

This collection:
	•	Appears in navigation only when it has content
	•	Is hidden automatically when empty

What You Must NOT Do

Do not:
	•	Manually add or remove damaged products from collections
	•	Create alternate navigation links for damaged items

Visibility is handled automatically to avoid broken links and empty pages.

⸻

When Something Feels “Off”

If you notice:
	•	A damaged product missing
	•	Inventory not matching expectations
	•	Copy that looks wrong
	•	A discount that doesn’t match the damage level

Correct Action
	1.	Do not edit the product
	2.	Do not create a workaround
	3.	Flag it internally (Slack / email)
	4.	Include:
	•	Product title
	•	Damage level
	•	What you expected vs. what you see

This allows fixes without corrupting the system.

⸻

Summary: Golden Rules

Always remember:
	•	Damaged products are system-managed
	•	Inventory is adjustable; structure is not
	•	Descriptions, discounts, and pricing are not editable
	•	Manual creation or editing causes long-term issues
	•	When in doubt: do nothing and flag it

This system is intentionally strict so that:
	•	You don’t have to guess
	•	Customers get clear, consistent information
	•	The catalog stays clean over time
